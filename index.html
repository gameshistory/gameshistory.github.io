Video Game History - Arcade, Consoles, and Home Computers

The objective of this website is to document the evolution of video game concepts in the public eye.

Notable exclusions are arcade prototypes, mainframe games, and text-based computer games.

Links include YouTube videos, Wikipedia articles, and Arcade Museum entries.

Hover over each link for information about a game or system.

Notes:

In the 1970s, Atari outnumbered any other developer four-to-one in terms of unique game concepts.
That said, it seems like the company ran into a bit of a creative block in 1977.
Dominos (1977) and Destroyer (1977) were rip-offs of Gremlin's Blockade and Depthcharge.
Likewise, Canyon Bomber (1977) was very similar to Meadow's Drop Zone 4.
This leads to the question of whether Drag Race (1977) was a copy of Electra's Eliminator IV.
Perhaps this is why Activision chose to rip-off Atari's Drag Race for their first game...

Questions:

Who made Safari? Taito or Gremlin?
Which came first? Atari's Drag Race (1977) or Electra's Eliminator IV (1976)?
Which came first? Flying Fortress [Electra] or Bomber [Sega]?
Which came first? Robot Bowling [Exidy] or Meadows Lanes [Meadows]?
Which came first? Space War [Leijac] or Space Beam [Irem]?
When was Space Chaser and Sheriff released? 1979 or 1980?
Did Seeburg's Deluxe Baseball really come out in 1974?

Star Fire should be listed as a 1979 game. It inspired Asteroids' leaderboard and obviously came out before Star Fire II (1979).
Space Invaders Galactica should say 1979 or 1980... That is, unless it really did come out before Galaxian (1979).
Ski (1975) [Allied Leisure Industries] is not a video game... At least, according to an active owner on Arcade Museum.
Why isn't Crossfire, Boxer, Castling, or Inferno in the Arcade Museum database?
